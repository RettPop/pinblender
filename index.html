Mechanism Instructions

The PinBlender uses a precise clockwork offset to hide your secret code within a stream of noise.

1. The Initial Gear Lag

To thwart brute-force attempts, the mechanism can prepend a random sequence of noise called Initial Gear Lag. This shifts the entire sequence, making the starting point of your PIN unpredictable.

2. The Position Offset

Your PIN digits are placed at intervals defined by the Position Offset. For an offset of 4, the mechanism generates 3 random digits, followed by your 1st PIN digit, and so on.

3. Trailing Noise

To mask the true length of your PIN, a random sequence of noise is appended at the very end.

4. Length Prefix

If engaged, a prefix like 4x is added to remind you that your original PIN was 4 digits long.

5. Unsecure Offset Exposure

WARNING: This feature exposes the PIN length, the Position Offset, and the Gear Lag directly in the output (e.g., 4@3:5x...). This significantly reduces security and is strictly for mechanical testing only.

6. Extracting the Essence

Provide an obfuscated sequence to retrieve the original PIN. If the sequence contains a prefix (like 4x or 4@3:5x), the mechanism will automatically calibrate. Otherwise, ensure both Position Offset and Initial Gear Lag match the ones used during obfuscation.

Example: PIN 1234, Offset 3
[XX]1[XX]2[XX]3[XX]4[XXXXXX...]

⚙️ Mechanical Failure! ⚙️

The gears have ground to a halt! The provided sequence does not align with our clockwork logic.

Ensure your input follows a recognized obfuscation pattern or matches the current Position Offset.
